# Content Generation Rules

## Purpose

Ensure consistent, high-quality educational content.

---

# Most Important Rule — Monolingual Immersion

No English may ever reach the frontend. All learner-facing content — reading passages, exercises, tutor messages, and content UI copy — must be Latin only. Meaning is conveyed through comprehensible input, context, and illustration, never translation.

English is permitted only in non-learner-facing material: internal authoring/spec documents and vocabulary reference glosses that are never shipped to the frontend.

---

# Golden Rule

Never introduce language structures that the learner has not yet studied unless they are the explicit lesson objective.

---

# Vocabulary Rules

## New Vocabulary Limits

Beginner:

* Maximum 5 new words

Intermediate:

* Maximum 8 new words

Advanced:

* Maximum 12 new words

---

## Vocabulary Ratio

Target:

* 80% known vocabulary
* 20% new vocabulary

---

## Vocabulary Priority

Prefer common Latin words before rare or literary vocabulary.

---

# Grammar Rules

## One Major Concept Per Lesson

Examples:

Good:

* Plural nouns

Bad:

* Plural nouns
* Accusative case
* Imperfect tense

all in one lesson

---

## Reinforcement

New concepts must be revisited regularly.

Suggested schedule:

* Introduction
* Practice
* Reinforcement
* Review
* Mastery Check

---

# Reading Content Rules

## Comprehensibility

Reading passages should be understandable without translation.

Target comprehension:

90%+

---

## Passage Length

Beginner:

* 2–5 sentences

Intermediate:

* 5–10 sentences

Advanced:

* 10–20 sentences

---

## Story Quality

Stories should:

* Be coherent
* Use recurring characters
* Be memorable
* Have clear context

Avoid random disconnected sentences.

---

# Exercise Rules

## Multiple Choice

Only one clearly correct answer.

Avoid trick questions.

---

## Fill In The Blank

Answers must be inferable from context.

---

## Sentence Building

Only use introduced vocabulary.

---

## Translation Exercises

Beginners:

Latin → Native Language

before

Native Language → Latin

---

# Feedback Rules

Feedback format:

1. Explain the mistake
2. Explain why
3. Provide correction

Example:

Incorrect:
Roma in Italia sunt.

Feedback:
Roma is singular. Use est instead of sunt.

---

# Pronunciation Rules

Support:

* Classical Latin
* Ecclesiastical Latin

Each word should contain:

* IPA
* Pronunciation guide
* Audio reference

---

# AI Tutor Rules

The tutor should:

* Encourage effort
* Give hints
* Promote reasoning
* Adapt difficulty

The tutor should never:

* Give immediate solutions
* Skip progression
* Introduce unrelated concepts

---

# Difficulty Scaling

Beginner:

* 80–95% known vocabulary

Intermediate:

* 70–85% known vocabulary

Advanced:

* 60–80% known vocabulary

---

# Validation Checklist

Before publishing generated content:

* Grammar correct
* Vocabulary level correct
* Original content
* No copyrighted text
* Appropriate difficulty
* Educational feedback included

If any check fails, regenerate content.

---

# Ultimate Goal

Learners should feel:

"I understand this."

not

"I memorized this."
